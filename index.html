Exploring the Impact of Remote Work on Employee Productivity

Your Research Question

How has the shift to remote work influenced employee productivity across different industries since 2020?

The Data You Will Use to Address Your Question

To address this question, I will analyze data from employee performance reports, productivity software usage logs, and employee surveys gathered from companies that transitioned to remote work. The data sources include:

  • Survey data from Gallup and Pew Research Center
  • Time-tracking software analytics from platforms like RescueTime and Toggl
  • Performance KPIs from companies before and after remote work adoption

An Explanation of the Data and Design Decisions You Made

I selected a mix of qualitative and quantitative data sources to capture a holistic view of productivity trends. Surveys provide subjective employee feedback, while time-tracking tools offer objective productivity metrics. Combining these allows for a balanced analysis that considers both perceptions and performance.

The design of the study ensures that data spans multiple industries to control for sector-specific variables. I chose data from 2020 onwards to focus on post-pandemic remote work trends. The decision to include performance KPIs helps ground the analysis in real business outcomes, rather than solely relying on self-reported data.
